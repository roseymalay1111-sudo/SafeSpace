Awareness & Prevention

Sexual harassment is any unwanted behavior of a sexual nature that makes someone feel uncomfortable, unsafe, or disrespected.

What It Can Look Like
  • ✔ Sexual comments, jokes, or messages
  • ✔ Unwanted touching or physical contact
  • ✔ Sexual images or gestures without consent
  • ✔ Staring or invading personal space
Important to Remember
  • ✔ Consent must be clear and ongoing
  • ✔ Silence does not mean agreement
  • ✔ Harassment is never the survivor’s fault
  • ✔ Support and reporting can prevent future harm

Creating awareness builds respectful communities and helps protect everyone’s dignity and safety.

Protect Your Private Areas

Everyone has private areas. Learning about body boundaries helps children and survivors stay safe and confident.

  • ✔ Private parts belong only to you
  • ✔ You can say No anytime
  • ✔ Trusted adults should be told if you feel unsafe
  • ✔ Your body deserves respect

Always trust your feelings and speak up if something feels wrong.

You deserve care, rest, and compassion. Click each card to learn gentle self-care practices.

Slow breathing helps regulate your nervous system and reduce anxiety.

Private Journaling

Writing thoughts privately can help release emotions without pressure.

Rest & Safety

Mindfulness & Meditation

Mindfulness helps reduce stress and brings attention to the present moment.

Creative Expression

Painting, music, or crafts can help process feelings safely and express emotions.
